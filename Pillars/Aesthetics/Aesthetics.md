# Aesthetics

## Overview

Aesthetics holds the visual identity and design language of Knowledge Islands - the brand system, symbolic vocabulary, and asset library
that give the concept a consistent, recognisable form across diagrams, interfaces, publications, and communications.

The visual language is grounded in the same metaphors that underpin the model itself: archipelagos, cartography, exploration, and the
stewardship of knowledge. Aesthetics is not decoration - it is the surface through which the model is understood and trusted.

---

## Visual Style

[[Visual Style/Visual Style|Visual Style]] defines the complete brand system for Knowledge Islands: the emotional tone, colour palette,
typography direction, illustration style, motion language, and the geographic and symbolic metaphors that carry consistent meaning across
all visual outputs. It includes the reusable master prompt for generating on-brand imagery.

---

## Symbol Library

[[Symbol Library/Symbol Library|Symbol Library]] documents the canonical set of visual symbols used across Knowledge Islands outputs. Each
symbol carries a defined conceptual meaning - Island, Archipelago, Harbour, Lighthouse, and so on - and is provided as a reference tile and
as standalone illustration artwork. The library ensures symbolic consistency across diagrams, presentations, and interfaces.

---

## Isometric Tiles

[[Isometric Tiles/Isometric Tiles|Isometric Tiles]] holds the isometric tile sets: terrain types (land, cliffs, coastal, ocean), structure
tiles (small buildings through to landmarks), connectors, props, and large composition examples. These are the building blocks for
constructing map-like visual representations of Knowledge Islands territories and architectures.

## Diagrams

[[Diagrams/Diagrams|Diagrams]] collects the canonical conceptual diagrams for Knowledge Islands - the system overview (The Cycle, Agents,
Geography), the territory map showing Arcadia and its satellite islands, and the Knowledge Hierarchy (Information, Knowledge, Wisdom). These
are the primary reference visuals for explaining the model.
