# Agents

Agents are the actors within the knowledge cycle - human or artificial - who create, shape, and move knowledge. They are the force of change
within the system.

**Human agents** originate meaning. They apply judgment, define value, and are the sole source of wisdom. Human agents interact with the
knowledge system through interfaces: notes, documents, editors, search, dashboards, visual maps. These support understanding, creativity,
reflection, and judgment - the distinctly human contributions.

**Artificial agents** extend scale, speed, and recall. They assist with capture, connection, and curation - but not reflection. Reflection
is the distinctly human stage of the cycle: reframing, judging, deciding what endures. Artificial agents interact through structured
protocols - tools such as MCP that allow them to discover knowledge sources, read from and propose changes to repositories, and operate
within defined jurisdictional boundaries.

Both participate in the same knowledge cycle. The system does not distinguish between them at the structural level - an edit is an edit.
What differs is the source of meaning: only human agents can determine whether a piece of knowledge is true, valuable, or worth keeping.

> Humans use interfaces. AI agents use protocols. Both participate in the same knowledge cycle.

Every agent holds a standing within each island they interact with - Visitor, Citizen, or Council Member - which determines their governance
role and what they can formally propose.
