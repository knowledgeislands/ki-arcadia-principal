# Governance

Governance is the operational layer that makes the island function day to day. It is not separate from the island - it is the mechanism through which the island's knowledge remains trustworthy, navigable, and durable across agents and over time. A well-governed island can be used reliably by both human and artificial agents without ambiguity; a poorly governed one accumulates inconsistency until it stops being usable.

Five areas work together to constitute governance.

**Conventions** define the shared language. They specify what a note looks like (format, sections, heading structure, markdown rules), what metadata it carries (frontmatter properties, tag taxonomy), where it lives (folder structure, routing rules), and what boundary separates internal knowledge in Pillars from external reference in Resources. Conventions are not stylistic - they are the mechanism through which notes remain processable by any agent, regardless of who wrote them or when.

**Processes** are the formal gates. The Enactment Process is the internal ratification gate: nothing becomes stable knowledge in Pillars or Resources without council review and ratification of a formal proposal. The Contribution Process is the external boundary: it governs how knowledge crosses island boundaries and how other archipelagos may propose changes to the canonical model held in Arcadia. Together they ensure that the Library's authority is maintained without becoming a bottleneck.

**Activities** are the ongoing work. Scheduled automations (daily briefings, email triage, Linear sync, health checks, memory rebuild) run without human initiation and prevent drift from accumulating. Conversational activities (inbox reviews, wikilink audits, status sweeps) run on a human-initiated cadence and handle judgement-heavy curation that automation cannot. Neither replaces the other; the island requires both. Activities divide into two categories: **constitutional** (required of any island adopting Knowledge Islands, not subject to the adoption framework) and **adoptable** (each territory explicitly adopts or vetoes each group, with no unknowns permitted).

**Agents** are everything that reads, writes, or reasons over island content - human or artificial. Human agents supply editorial judgement, originate meaning, and hold ratification authority. Artificial agents extend scale, speed, and recall, handling routine and repeatable work. The operating conventions for each agent type - what it can do, how it behaves, what patterns it follows - are documented separately from the tools it uses.

**Tools** are the editors, AI systems, task managers, and connected services through which agents act. The tool layer covers configuration and connection; the agent layer covers operating behaviour. Neither duplicates the other.
