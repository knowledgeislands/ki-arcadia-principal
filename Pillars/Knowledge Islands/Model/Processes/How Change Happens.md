# How Change Happens

## Overview

The Processes chapter holds the operational mechanisms through which governance is enacted in practice. These are not policies or
conventions - they are the active procedures that move knowledge from idea to ratified content, and that govern how the Knowledge Islands
model itself propagates across the ecosystem.

Two distinct processes are documented here. The **Enactment Process** is internal: it governs how any island moves a proposal through its
own council to ratification. The **Contribution Process** is external: it governs how knowledge developed in one archipelago can be proposed
as a canonical addition to the shared Knowledge Islands model. Both processes work in concert with [[Governance|Governance]], which defines
who counts as a Citizen, what council membership means, and what authority the council holds.

---

## Enactment Process

The Enactment Process is the internal change mechanism for a Knowledge Island. It describes the stages a proposal passes through - from
draft to council review to ratification - and the responsibilities of each participant at each stage. Any change to island content that
requires formal approval passes through this process.

## Contribution Process

The Contribution Process is the external propagation mechanism: the procedure by which knowledge developed in one island or archipelago can
be proposed as a canonical addition to the shared Knowledge Islands model. It is the route through which the portable model grows, and the
check that prevents island-specific content from contaminating the shared standard.
