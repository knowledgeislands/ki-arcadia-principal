# Constitutional

## Overview

Constitutional activities are in a different category from all other activity groups. They are not subject to the adoption framework — a territory does not adopt or veto them. They are the baseline that defines what it means to be a Knowledge Island at all. A territory without them is not running Knowledge Islands; it is an unstructured repository that borrows the vocabulary.

This distinction matters because the adoption model otherwise treats every activity group symmetrically: a territory must take an explicit position on each group (adopted or vetoed), and the Conformance Check flags any group without a declared position as non-conformant. Constitutional activities sit outside this model. They are verified by the Conformance Check, but they cannot appear in the Manifest's adoption table — they predate it.

Three elements are constitutional, spanning both activities and Knowledge Capital configuration:

- **Identity** — the island must have a named, populated Identity note in its Knowledge Capital. Without it, the island cannot be distinguished from any other.
- **Manifest** — the island must have a Manifest in its Knowledge Capital declaring its adoption position on every non-constitutional activity group. Without it, the adoption state is unknown and Conformance cannot proceed.
- **Conformance** — the island must run the Conformance Check. This is the sole constitutional *activity*, and it is self-grounding: the mechanism that verifies constitutional compliance is itself constitutional.

---

## [[Conformance]]

The Conformance Check is the sole activity in this group. It verifies that the island meets its constitutional baseline — Identity, Manifest, and Conformance itself — and that every non-constitutional activity group has an explicit adopted or vetoed position in the Manifest, with the required Knowledge Capital configuration present for adopted groups. It runs on a scheduled cadence and cannot be vetoed.
