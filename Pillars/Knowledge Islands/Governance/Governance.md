# Governance

## Overview

The Governance subtree holds the methodology, conventions, tooling, and maintenance activities that govern the island. Island-specific identity and routing configuration lives in [[Pillars/Knowledge Capital/Knowledge Capital|Knowledge Capital]].

---

## Conventions

The conventions that govern how the island operates — note format, frontmatter standards, folder structure, routing rules, and boundary enforcement — are documented in [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Conventions/Structure/Structure|Structure]] and its sub-notes.

See [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Conventions/Notes/Notes|Notes]] for the canonical note structure, and [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Processes/Contribution Process|Contribution Process]] for the rules governing knowledge at the boundary.

---

## Processes

The formal processes by which knowledge changes are proposed, reviewed, and ratified are documented in [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Processes/Processes|Processes]]. The [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Processes/Enactment Process|Enactment Process]] governs how council members bring changes to the Library; the [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Processes/Contribution Process|Contribution Process]] governs how external contributions are received and assessed.

---

## Activities

The ongoing work that keeps the island healthy — daily automations, weekly manual tasks, and per-session AI digests — is documented in [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Activities/Activities|Activities]].

---

## Agents

The operating layer — how humans and AI agents work within and against the island — is documented in [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Agents/Agents|Agents]]. Two modes are covered: human operation (manual curation, navigation, and review) and agentic AI operation (patterns, conventions, and memory).

---

## Tools

The tools that agents use — editors, AI systems, task managers, and connected services — are documented in [[Pillars/Knowledge Islands/Governance/Tools/Tools|Tools]]. Each tool note covers its purpose, configuration, and the conventions specific to working with it.
