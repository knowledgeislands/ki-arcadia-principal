# Layers of Knowledge

Knowledge exists across multiple layers, each extending its reach and lifespan.

**Individual knowledge** resides in the mind — extended through tools, notes, and systems into what is sometimes called a "second brain". Personal, contextual, and evolving.

**Collective knowledge** is shared across teams, communities, and organisations. It requires structure and shared understanding to remain coherent — without these, it fragments back into individual knowledge held by different people with no common ground.

**Civilisational knowledge** is preserved across generations: libraries, archives, cultural traditions, and the accumulated record of human understanding. It enables continuity — each generation builds on what came before rather than rediscovering it.

> Civilisation advances by retaining and building upon knowledge, not by rediscovering it.

Knowledge Islands operates at all three layers. A personal island (a territory of one) extends individual knowledge. A multi-island territory enables collective knowledge. The Knowledge Islands model itself — and Arcadia as its canonical home — is a contribution to civilisational knowledge.
