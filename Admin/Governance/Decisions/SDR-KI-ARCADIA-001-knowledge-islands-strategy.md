# SDR-KI-ARCADIA-001: Knowledge Islands — The Strategy

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-06-25

## Context

Knowledge accumulates, but accumulation alone is not understanding. The prevailing approaches to personal and organisational knowledge
management — filing systems, wikis, note-taking tools — treat knowledge as a storage problem. They answer "where does this go?" but not "how
does this relate to everything else?", "who holds authority over it?", or "how does it endure beyond any single person?". The result is
isolated silos: each person, team, or domain develops its own practice, disconnected from others, with no shared model for how knowledge
should be structured, governed, or evolved.

The emergence of LLM-native tools — AI systems that can read, connect, and act within a knowledge base — opens a different possibility.
Knowledge can be organised not as a filing system but as a living structure: governed, connected, and designed to extend beyond the limits
of any single mind. This requires a model, not just a tool.

## Decision

Knowledge Islands is adopted as the organising strategy. It treats knowledge not as a filing problem but as a civilisation question: who
creates it, who holds authority over it, how it travels, and how it endures. The model provides a geographic framework — the island as the
home of knowledge, the territory as the unit of governance, the archipelago as the structure that forms when territories connect — and a
jurisdictional and governance layer that makes it practical day to day.

The strategic intent is an archipelago of Knowledge Islands, each owning a distinct domain of human concern:

| Domain      | Island    | Concern                               |
| ----------- | --------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Knowledge   | Arcadia   | Cultivation, integration, learning    |
| Justice     | Equitas   | Fairness, equity, proportionality     |
| Governance  | Concordia | Harmony, cooperation, civic structure |
| Wisdom      | Athenae   | Judgement, strategy, understanding    |
| Truth       | Aletheia  | Discovery, revelation, clarity        |
| Stewardship | Themis    | Order, continuity, guardianship       |
| Restoration | Harmonia  | Healing, reconciliation, renewal      |

Each island is introduced independently when its time comes. The archipelago is the scope of the strategy, not a delivery commitment.

The Knowledge Island of Knowledge Islands — **Arcadia** — is the first realisation and the canonical home of the portable model from which
every island that adopts Knowledge Islands derives its baseline.

## Consequences

- The Knowledge Islands model governs how all islands are structured, governed, and evolved.
- Arcadia holds the canonical, portable definition of the model. New islands derive from Arcadia, not from one another.
- Each domain is introduced by adding an island, not by extending an existing one. Islands are independent: their own repos, their own
  governance, their own territory.
- The strategy is long-horizon and directional. Individual islands are introduced by separate decisions as each domain matures.
