# Introduction Act Overview

## Overview

The Introduction establishes the philosophical and conceptual foundation for the Knowledge Islands model before the operational detail of
the Model act begins. It answers the question: why does this exist? Two chapters work in sequence - Background builds from first principles
to the central reveal; Concept names the key actors and structures the reader will encounter throughout.

The full operational specification - conventions, processes, activities, agents, and tools - is covered in [[Model/Model|Model]]. Each
island's specific realisation lives in its [[Knowledge Capital]]. Every generic concept defined in Knowledge Islands may have a
corresponding specific realisation in Knowledge Capital; the two are structural mirrors. Knowledge Islands holds the portable,
island-agnostic definition; Knowledge Capital holds Arcadia's instance. When adding a new concept to Knowledge Islands, any island-specific
counterpart belongs in Knowledge Capital, not here.

---

## Background

The Background chapter builds from first principles to the central reveal of the model - that knowledge needs a home, not just a filing
system. It moves through the history of knowledge systems, the nature of knowledge itself, the layers knowledge occupies, other approaches
and why they fall short, and the mechanism that Knowledge Islands proposes. The chapter is structured as a narrative arc, each section
preparing the ground for the next.

## Concept

The Concept chapter introduces the actors and structures that the model operates through: territories and archipelagos as the scale model,
agents as the operating layer, jurisdiction as the authority structure, and governance as the mechanism of ratified change. These concepts
are prerequisites for reading the Model act.
